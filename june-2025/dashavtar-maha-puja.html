About The Puja

Dashavtar represents the 10 avatars of Lord Vishnu. ‘Dasha’ means ten and ‘Avatar’ means incarnation. Matsya, Koorma, Varaha, and Narasimha in Satya Yuga, Vaaman, Parasurama and Sri Ram in Treta Yuga, Balaram and Shri Krishna in Dwapar Yuga, Kalki Avtar which is about to come in Kali Yuga.

Why Is This Puja For You?

  • Dashavtar Maha Puja is done to gain positivity, health, wealth, security, prosperity, wisdom, courage, strength, growth, abundance and gains. It also eliminates evil, negative, and demonic forces from life.
  • This puja will be conducted on 6th June at 1:50 PM (IST) as per Shubh Muhurat which will last for 3 to 4 Hours by 7 Brahmins to seek blessings.
  • This Puja involves Vedic rituals like Dashavtar Bhagwan Maha Abhishek, Chanting Beej Mantra, and 108 Names of God Vishnu along with other important rituals.
  • Your Sankalp will be taken as per your full name and Gotra as guided in our Vedic scriptures. You will also receive a small video separately through WhatsApp in which our Vedic Pandit will be chanting your name and Gotra.

Steps of the Puja

Shanti Paath, Devata Namaskar, Maha Sankalp, Ganga Pujan, Prithvi Pujan, Deepak Pujan, Surya Pujan, Dasho Disha Puajn, Hanuman Pujan, Bhairav Pujan, Kuldevi Pujan, Pitru Namaskar, Guru Vandana, Ganapathi Pujan, Maha Abhishek ( Ganesh, Lakshmi, shiv and Dasho Avtar ), Vishnu Sahasranam paath, Tulsi Archan, Purusha Suktam Paath, Aarti, Pushpanjali, Kshama Prarthana, Maha Prasad, Ashirvad, Brahman Bhojan.

Nav Kanya Puja, also known as Kanya Pujan or Kanjak Pujan, is a ritual performed during the festival of Navratri. It involves worshiping nine young girls, usually below the age of puberty, symbolizing the nine forms of the goddess Durga.

  • Yajaman offers them food and gifts. Also, seek their blessings as a way of honoring the divine feminine energy and acknowledging their purity and strength. This ritual is a significant part of Navratri celebrations and is believed to bring blessings, prosperity, and spiritual growth.
  • This beautiful ritual involves Kanya Paduka Pujan (washing of the feet), Kanya Pujan, and Nav Kanya Bhojan. This puja is held on the 9th day of Navratri will be conducted by 1 Vedic pandit.
